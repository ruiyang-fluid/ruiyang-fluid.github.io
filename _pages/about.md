I am currently an assistant professor at the School of Aerospace Engineering and Center for Combustion Energy at Tsinghua University. I received my Ph.D. and M.S. degree in in the Physics of Fluids group with Prof. Detlef Lohse at the University of Twente in 2023 and 2020, and my bachelor’s degree in Theoretical and Applied Mechanics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2018. Prior to current position, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher from 2024 to 2026 in Prof. Luc Deike's group at the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, Princeton University. 

My research lies in the theoretical and numerical modeling of multiphase and multi-physics fluid flows, addressing coupled problems involving phase change, interfacial transport, and turbulent flows. Core applications include ice melting and freezing, phase-change thermal management, and in-situ manufacturing in complex environments.

I have published more than twenty peer-reviewed papers in journals such as Phys. Rev. Lett., J. Fluid Mech., Phys. Rev. Fluids, and PRX Energy. I have received WHOI Geophysical Fluid Dynamics (GFD) Fellowship 2022, Cum Laude distinction for PhD 2023, ECCOMAS Best PhD Thesis nominees 2023, Ehrenfest-Afanassjewa thesis award (Honourable mention) by Dutch Physics Council 2023, and invited talk at 9th ECCOMAS Congress, Portugal.
