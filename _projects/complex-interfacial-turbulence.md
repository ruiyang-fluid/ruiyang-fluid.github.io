I study turbulent flows in which interfaces actively shape transport, instability, and coherent dynamics. This includes two-layer and double-diffusive convection, bubbles attached to heated plates, surfactant effects on gravity-capillary waves, coalescing wall-attached bubbles, wind-forced breaking waves, liquid-jet instability, and data-driven analysis of turbulent flow structures. The common thread is the coupling between turbulence, interfacial physics, and multiscale transport.

Related publications include work on heat transfer in two immiscible fluid layers, turbulent convection with attached bubbles, surfactant-modified gravity-capillary waves, detachment of coalescing wall-attached bubbles, multilayer simulations of breaking wave fields, liquid-jet instabilities, double-diffusive convection, and data-driven identification of spatio-temporal turbulent structures.
